## Goal
Deliver a fully installable PWA on iOS Safari and Android Chrome: proper PNG icons in all required sizes, iOS splash screen support, and a manifest that passes the Lighthouse PWA installability audit.

## Why it matters
The app's primary distribution channel is "Add to Home Screen." If the manifest or icons are misconfigured, the install prompt never fires on Android and the iOS experience degrades (no splash screen, generic icon). This is the gate for end-user delivery.

## Scope
- Generate PNG icons from existing SVG designs: 192×192, 512×512, 180×180 apple-touch-icon
- Produce a maskable 512×512 PNG with content in the safe zone
- Update the vite-plugin-pwa manifest to reference PNG icons with explicit sizes
- Add `orientation: 'portrait'`, `id: '/'`, `categories: ['utilities']` to the manifest
- Add `<link rel="apple-touch-startup-image">` tags for major iOS device viewports
- Verify `storage.persist()` is called at app init (ADR-002 follow-up)

## Out of scope
- Offline caching strategy (T-041)

## Implementation notes
- Use `@vite-pwa/assets-generator` with the `minimal-2023` preset to generate PNG icons from the existing `public/icon-512.svg` source
- The maskable icon safe zone is the inner 80% of the image; the existing SVG letter "B" may be clipped — verify or adjust safe-zone padding in the SVG before generating
- Apple splash screens for MVP: use generated placeholder images at the 5 most common iPhone sizes (375×812, 390×844, 414×896, 428×926, 430×932) plus iPad (768×1024, 1024×1366)
- `storage.persist()` must be called once at startup, not on every render; place in main.tsx after mount

## Acceptance criteria
- `public/` contains `icon-192.png`, `icon-512.png`, `apple-touch-icon.png`, and apple-touch-startup-image files for at least 5 device sizes
- The vite-plugin-pwa manifest lists PNG icons with correct `sizes` and `purpose` fields
- `index.html` contains `<link rel="apple-touch-icon">` referencing a 180×180 PNG and at least 5 `apple-touch-startup-image` link tags
- `vite build` completes without errors or manifest warnings
- `storage.persist()` is called at app init in `main.tsx`
